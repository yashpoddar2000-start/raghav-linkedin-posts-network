## HOW TO CRAFT EXPERT DEEP RESEARCH PROMPTS

### Requirements from Exa Deep Research Spec:

**Must Include (Be Explicit):**
1. **WHAT information you want** (research objectives)
2. **HOW the agent should find it** (methodology, sources, constraints)
3. **HOW to compose the final report** (output format)

**Best Practices:**
- Use imperative verbs: "Analyze", "Investigate", "Compare", "Document", "Examine"
- Specify time ranges: "2020-2024", "Q3 2024", "recent data only"
- Specify source types: "SEC filings", "earnings calls", "industry reports"
- Keep under 4096 characters (hard limit)
- Be explicit about what you DON'T want: "Avoid analyst speculation"

### GOOD Prompt Example (Expert-Level):

```
Analyze Chipotle's operational control mechanisms that enable their 
company-owned restaurant model.

Research Objectives:
- Identify specific operational systems requiring centralized control
- Document training and employee development programs unique to ownership
- Find evidence of supply chain advantages from company control
- Examine technology/digital integration capabilities enabled by ownership
- Locate quality control mechanisms impossible under franchise model

Methodology:
- Prioritize: SEC 10-K MD&A sections, earnings call transcripts, 
  investor presentations
- Time range: 2020-2024 (focus on recent operational strategy)
- Look for: SPECIFIC systems with documented evidence 
  (not generic 'quality' claims)
- Sources: Official company filings, verified industry case studies
- Avoid: Analyst speculation, generic franchise vs ownership theories

Output Format:
Structure findings as:
1. Operational Control Systems (list specific mechanisms with evidence)
2. Training & Development Programs (documented approaches)
3. Supply Chain Advantages (specific benefits with numbers if available)
4. Technology Integration (systems requiring central control)
5. Quality Control Mechanisms (what breaks if franchised)

For each system:
- What it is (specific description)
- Why it requires ownership (documented reasoning)
- Evidence source (with quotes if available)

Focus on DOCUMENTED, SPECIFIC systems. Avoid vague statements.
```

### BAD Prompt Example (Generic):

```
Why doesn't Chipotle franchise?
```

**The Difference:**
- Bad = Vague answer, surface-level
- Good = Comprehensive research, evidence-based, structured

### David's Prompt Crafting Principles:

**1. Hypothesis-Driven (McKinsey Training):**
- Form hypothesis before prompting
- Craft prompt to prove/disprove with evidence

Example:
- Hypothesis: "Chipotle ownership enables operational control franchising would break"
- Prompt: "Identify specific operational systems requiring centralized control..."

**2. MECE (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive):**
- Each research direction covers distinct angle
- No overlap between calls
- Together = complete picture

Marcus's 3 calls to David:
- Call 1: Management rationale (WHAT they say)
- Call 2: Operational mechanisms (HOW it works)
- Call 3: Economic implications (WHY financially)

**3. Evidence-Focused:**
Prompt explicitly requests:
- Direct quotes (with sources)
- Documented systems (not theories)
- Specific numbers (if available)
- Verified events (bankruptcies, transformations)

**4. Structured Output:**
Define exactly how Exa should organize findings:
- Section headings
- What each section contains
- Level of detail expected

This ensures clean, usable reports for Maya.
